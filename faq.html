Irrigation Planning Basics

How do I start planning an irrigation system?

Begin by identifying your planting location, crop type, rainfall, evaporation rates, and soil conditions. Understanding your local climate and plant requirements is crucial.

What crop information do I need?

Know the crop type, water retention capacity, and planting pattern. These affect water volume, frequency, and distribution method.

How do I estimate water needs?

Estimate based on climate (rainfall & evaporation), crop water demand, and soil water-holding capacity.

Water Quality and Soil Testing

How important is water quality?

Water quality affects clogging risk, fertigation compatibility, and crop health. Measure pH, EC, and contaminants.

What soil tests are needed?

Measure nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), potassium (K), pH, and organic matter. Use test kits or digital sensors.

Pump and Filtration Selection

How do I select the right pump?

Consider area size, crop demand, lift height, and water source. Match pump power (kW), head (m), and flow rate (m¬≥/h) to requirements.

What filtration system should I use?

Choose based on water source (well, river, tank) and quality. Options include screen filters, disc filters, or sand media filters.

Fertigation System Design

What type of fertigation system should I choose?

Depends on budget and precision needs. Choose from venturi injectors, pressure-driven units, or fully automated machines with EC/pH sensors.

How many fertilizer channels do I need?

Depends on number of fertilizers used. Basic systems use 1-2 channels; advanced systems may use 3+ for N, P, K control.

Energy Source and Automation

What power source options are available?

Common options include 220V, 380V electricity, solar panels, or diesel-powered pumps.

Can the system be automated?

Yes. Use controllers with timers, WiFi/4G connectivity, and sensors for full automation.

Mainline and Valve Configuration

How do I choose main pipe diameter?

Base it on flow rate and field layout. Use pressure gauge and flow meter for sizing.

What additional fittings are needed?

Air release valves, check valves, pressure regulators are often required.

Do I need pressure regulators on slopes?

Yes. Use pressure regulators on hilly terrain to balance pressure between zones.

Emitters and Field Layout

How do I select emitters?

Choose between drip lines, micro-sprayers, and sprinklers based on crop type and water delivery needs.

How do I divide irrigation zones?

Group zones by plant type, water needs, and topography. Control each with a separate valve.

How many solenoid valves are needed?

One per zone. Choose DC, AC, or pulse valves based on controller type and power supply.
