Understanding Electric Valve Power Ratings in Smart Irrigation Systems

Author: PKYDrip Tech Team | Published: April 2025

🔍 Common Question:

“My client says the electric valve is 6kW, but the supplier says it’s only 45W. Who is right?”

✅ Quick Answer:

This is a classic misunderstanding. A typical motorized electric valve used in irrigation systems usually consumes around 45W to 100W. If someone reports a power requirement of 6kW, they may be:

  • Mistaking the total power required for running dozens of valves simultaneously
  • Referring to a pump rather than the valve itself
  • Receiving an estimate from a non-technical party without accurate calculations

💡 How to Calculate Total Power Usage

If your irrigation system uses 10 motorized valves at the same time, and each consumes 50W:

50W x 10 valves = 500W total power required

Always add a safety margin of 10–20%, especially when sizing your power supply or inverter.

⚙️ What About 6kW?

6kW is a very large figure for valves. It’s more likely to be the power rating of a submersible or centrifugal water pump, commonly used in large-scale farms or greenhouses. Pumps have motors that run continuously, while valves are intermittent and much lower in power draw.
