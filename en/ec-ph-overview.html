Understanding EC and pH in Fertigation Systems

In modern agriculture, managing the balance of water and nutrients is essential to improving yield and resource efficiency. Two of the most critical indicators for nutrient solutions are EC (Electrical Conductivity) and pH (acidity/alkalinity).

EC measures the electrical conductivity of a nutrient solution. It indicates the total dissolved salts (TDS), which correlate directly to the fertilizer concentration. High EC means more salts, while low EC may result in nutrient deficiency.

pH measures how acidic or alkaline a solution is. Most crops prefer a pH between 5.5 and 6.5. If pH is too low or too high, certain nutrients become unavailable to plants, even if they are present in the solution.
